2015

The first iteration of CodeLM was really more of an experiment. It took place on Thursday, May 28th in the Lower Merion High School library and was only open to LMHS students. Based on the success of the event and the positive feedback, we decided to hold CodeLM again in 2016.

2016

Our second event took place on February 9th and grew in many ways. We opened CodeLM up to other schools in the area and 10 schools signed up. We also gained a corporate sponsor, Susquehanna International Group, who sent some developers to help with the event. Finally, we introduced an open-ended problem that was hand-graded by the SIG developers. Competitors were tasked with implementing a modified version of Blackjack that we invented called Diecisiete. (First intro video)

2017

The third event took place on March 8th. The dashboard was rewritten from scratch in Django. (Second intro video)

2018

Our fourth event took place on April 17th. We moved from the Lower Merion High School library to SIG's headquarters in Bala Cynwyd.

2019

Our fifth event (and first major milestone) took place on March 29th. We had so many competitors that we had to spread out across two floors of the SIG building!

2020

Our sixth event took place on April 17th. Due to the coronavirus pandemic, we were forced to move the event online, but this didn't come without benefits. Because competitors didn't have to show up in person, we were able to open up the event to students all across the country. Over 200 students from 14 states competed - far more than any previous year.

2021

Our seventh event is coming some time in Spring 2021 and will most likely be online again. Check back soon for more details!
